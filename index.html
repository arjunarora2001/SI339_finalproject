Welcome to my website about the game of cricket! Here I will be giving everyone a brief introduction on the 9 best teams in cricket history. Click on any nation's flag to learn more!

The Indian Cricket Team

The Indian cricket team is historically one of the most successful and feared cricket teams in the world (and no, there is obviously no bias here).
  • Also known as the "Men in Blue".
  • Have won two ODI world cups (1983, 2011).
  • Have won one T20 world cup (2007).
  • Home to the most successful franchise cricket league in the world - Indian Premier League (IPL), which contributes almost $200m to the nation's GDP.
  • Historic rivalry with Pakistan which dates back decades.
Notable Players

Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar, born on April 24th, 1973, is a former Indian cricketer who is widely regarded as the greatest player the world has ever seen. Revered and respected across the globe for his consistency in all formats of the game, he is most fondly remembered for his picture perfect cover drives. Dubbed the "God of Cricket", his following extends beyond just Indian fans and his aura radiates across the sport even today. He is, as the kids say, the undisputed GOAT.

Yuvraj Singh

Yuvraj Singh, born on December 12th, 1981 is one the best all-rounders India has seen, popular for his spinning abilities in the bowling innings, and destructive six-hitting potential in the batting innings. The lore of Yuvraj Singh, however, extends beyond his genius on the field. Diagnosed with cancer in 2011, his grit and ability to never give in defined his image. Not only would he defeat his illness, he would, in 2011 itself, play a key role in India's World Cup triumph, taking home the Player of the Tournament award. An inspiration to many, he more than left his mark on all of the 1.4 billion Indians' hearts that watched him play, and has cemented his legacy in Indian cricket for eternity. Yuvi Paaji, as he is called by many, is remembered most perhaps for his iconic "Six Sixes" against England in 2007, when he became the first player ever to hit six sixes of all six balls of an over, that to against one of the most feared bowlers at the time: Stuart Broad. Motivated by an altercation with one of the other England players, he reminded the world what happens when you mess with India and when you mess with Yuvraj Singh.

The Australian Cricket Team

The Australian national cricket team is the joint oldest team in the world, speaking volumes to the accolades they have garnered over the years. The most succesfull cricket team to date, they have been feared all across the world, having a reputation for housing some of the world's most destructive batsmen, athletic fielders, and deadly bowlers - the whole package!
  • Also known as the "Aussies".
  • Have won a record of five ODI world cups (1987, 1999, 2003, 2007, 2015).
  • Have won one T20 world cup (2021).
  • Historic rivalry with England which dates back decades. Their match-up is famously known as "The Ashes".
Notable Players

Sir Don Bradman

Donald George Bradman predates any cricketer from the modern age. "The Don," as he is famously called, never played any ICC-regulated cricket. From world cups to franchise cricket, every tournament that is part of the foundation of the sport today came after Sir Don. Yet, they say there will never be anyone like him ever again. The holder of one of the most ridiculous record in all of sports, this behemoth of the game boasts a batting average of 99.94. Although records are meant to be broken, it is safe to say that this one will stand the test of time because it is simply unbeatable. He famously got out for 0 when he needed only four runs in his last ever game from Australia to achieve an average of 100. Nevertheless, his commitment to excellence and entertainment will never be forgotten and his place within the highest echelons of the game will never be questioned.

Shane Warne

On 4th March, 2022, the world mourned the loss of one Shane Warne. There aren't many cricketers one would put above Shane Warne when listing out the all time greats, and in an era where leg spin was starting to fade out of the game, the ultimate Aussie brought it back in a way only he could: with perfect precision. During his peak in the late 90s, Warne would lift the World Cup as vice-captain of Australia, putting in man of the match performances in the semis and the finals as well as ending the tournament as the highest wicket taker. His rivalry with Sachin Tendulkar is part of cricket folklore and their battles will not be forgotten for. A fierce competitor, with an even stronger character, the world will not only Shane Warne the player, but Warney too, the bloke from Victoria.

The English Cricket Team

The English national cricket represents England and Wales in international cricket, being one of the oldest and most iconic teams in the sport. They are the current holders of both the ODI and T20 world cups.
  • Have won one ODI world cup (2019).
  • Have won two T20 world cups (2010, 2022).
  • Historic rivalry with Australia which dates back decades. Their match-up is famously known as "The Ashes".
  • Once played a test game that lasted 14 days!
Notable Players

Sir Alastair Cook

Alastair Cook is a blast from the past. A traditional opener in every sense of the word, he was notoriously difficult to dismiss for even the best in the world. The fifth highest run scorer in test history and England's highest capped test player, he was a specialist who seemed to, at times, be playing at a level far higher than that of his colleagues. He further enhanced his reputation as one of the most successful captains of all time, captaining the England test side more than any other player before or since. Although his major achievements did come in test cricket, you'd be a fool to underestimate his quality. Sir Alastair Cook might very well be one of the most underrated cricketers out there, and one can only hope history does him justice.

Ben Stokes

Born in New Zealand, made in England, Ben Stokes is one of the best players in the world today. Unlike others on that particular list, he is the only one who boasts expert skills in bowling, batting and fielding. An all-rounder of the highest quality, his comeback story is one of the greatest in the modern game. In 2016, he conceded 24 runs in 4 balls in the last over of the T20 World Cup finals as the West Indies snatched the trophy from the arms of the English. A year later, in a tour against the West Indies, Stokes would get involved in a bar brawl and be found guilty. He'd lose his biggest sponsorships and be temporarily suspended. But such is the grit and determination of Stokes, he would come back in spectacular fashion. He'd be the standout player in England's 2019 ODI and 2021 T20 World Cup wins and today, England are the kings of the cricketing world and Ben Stokes is all the way at the top. Nevertheless, his greatest moment ever is hitting the winning runs in one of the greatest Ashes matches ever. They say he could have batted for five more days and not gotten out. Sit back and enjoy this masterclass.

The Bangladeshi Cricket Team

The Bangladeshi cricket team are a relatively new power in the cricketing sphere, captained by Shakib Al Hasan in tests and T20s, and Tanim Iqbal in ODIs. Although they are not the most consistent in their results, they are known to be able to cause upsets at any point. Their team has changed drastically in the past few years and gained the respect of other nations in their recent world cup outings!
  • Also known as "The Tigers".
  • Became a full member of the Cricket Association in 2000.
  • Holds the record for most consecutive losses in tests (21) and ODIs (23).
  • Have improved exponentially in the past decade, now becoming a stronghold cricketing nation.
The Heart of Bangladeshi Cricket

Shakib Al Hasan

Every year most cricket fans are faced with the same question: How is Shakib Al Hasan still playing? A man who is seemingly always moments away from retirement, Shakib Al Hasan has found a way to stay at the top of the game and keep Bangladesh competitive for over a decade and a half. Bangladesh, who for the past few years have oscillated between being a minnow and a top dog, would not have achieved the heights they have without their captain and main man. Over the years, he has proven his worth with the bat and ball, earning the status of one of the best all-rounders in the world. His consistency and longevity is almost unmatched and while he holds virtually every notable record for the Bangladesh national team, he also holds the very impressive record of being the first and only player to be the no.1 ranked all-rounder in all three formats of the game. Even in the twilight of his career, Shakib had a stellar 2019 ODI World Cup performance in which Bangladesh, as they have done so often in the past decade, surpassed all expectations, taking down giants of the game in West Indies and South Africa.

The Sri Lankan Cricket Team

The Sri Lankan national cricket team are a historic team, rising from underdog status to causing a great upset and winning the ODI world cup in 1996.
  • Also known as "The Lions".
  • Have won the ODI world cup once (1996).
  • Have won the T20 world cup once (2014).
  • Holds the world record for highest team total in test cricket (952 runs against India).
Notable Players / Moments

Muttiah Muralitharan

You simply cannot hate Muttiah Muralitharan. A globally admired superstar, Muralitharan is one of the nicest men to ever play the game. Nevertheless, beware! Behind that sweet smile is a devilishly deceptive googly and a near-impossible to read doosra. To date, the bowler with the highest international wickets of all time, his lore. Muralitharan was a key cog in a Sri Lankan team looking to finally establish themselves as a team that can compete at the very top. Besides being part of Sri Lanka's only ever World Cup win in 1996, he put the team on the map, at many times carrying them single-handedly through games. His list of records is enviable and the respect he garners within the game is unrivaled. He is simply one of the greatest to ever do it.

The Golden Era

If the Sri Lankan's of the 90s were the pioneers, the Sri Lankans of the late 2000s and the early 2010s were the golden era. A team of 11 world class athletes, it is almost impossible to pick one standout player. Each individual brought their own unique style as this team not only broke the Pakistan-India duopoly in Asia, but also conquered the world. From 2004 to 2014, this team won three Asia Cups and a T20 World Cup. During that time, they would also finish as runners up at a further two T20 World Cups and at 2 ODI World Cups. Although some might argue this team underachieved, the fear they struck into the hearts of their opponents cannot be understated. From Dilshan's “Pallu Scoop” to Malinga's “Slinga”, they displayed flair and quality in equal amounts. Sangakkara, Jayawardane and the evergreen Muralitharan brought experience, longevity and leadership in a way that few have in the history of the sport. Perera and Mathews could do it all in the middle too. The golden era of Sri Lankan cricket may never be replicated, but we can rest easy in the knowledge that they will never be forgotten.

The New Zealand Cricket Team

The New Zealand national cricket team - one of the scariest teams to face due to their lethal bowling attack and explosive batsmen. They are unfortunately, commonly known as the unluckiest cricket team, as they have often failed to perform on the big stage, such as playoff games, in spite of great performances in league stages.
  • Also known as "The Black Caps".
  • Have never lifted world cup silverware in spite of being one of the most feared teams in the world.
  • Often known as the humblest and most kind teams, due to their unwavering sportsmanship and gestures on the field.
Notable Players / Moments

Ross Taylor

New Zealand's highest run scorer of all time, Ross Taylor is one of the greats of the game. His expertise in the longer formats of the game is well-respected and, as the cliché goes with New Zealand players, he went about his business without much fuss. Along with being one of the longest standing captains of the Kiwis, he became the first cricketer to play 100 games in all three formats of the game. As well-rounded as they come, his ability to adapt his game to the situation and make himself a valuable member of any team until the very end of his career is often overlooked by the mainstream media. Although he will most probably lose the mantle of highest run-scorer for New Zealand to the next player on our list, his contribution to the national team will be fondly remembered.

Kane Williamson

New Zealand are known today as the “good guys” of cricket. Regardless of whether or not they would use that tag to describe themselves, it is one that has been given to them by the cricketing world, and nobody embodies that idea more than their captain: Kane Williamson. Born in 1990, he is the first captain to ever win a major ICC honor for New Zealand. Perennial underdogs at virtually every tournament they've played in, New Zealand made a habit of wowing audiences before eventually, always falling short. Under the calm and composed leadership of Williamson, however, they lifted the inaugural World Test Championship in 2021 against the no.1 ranked test team in the world: India. Part of the infamous, “Fab 4”, a term used to refer to the four best and most consistent batsmen in the game today, Williamson might not be the flashiest player, but he is by far one of the most respected.

The West Indies Cricket Team

The West Indies cricket team comprises of multiple nations spanning the countries and territories in the Caribbean region. They are popularly known as the most entertaining team to watch due to their explosive batting history and carnage in every T20 tournament they partake in. From the 1970s to the 1990s, they were consolidated as the strongest team in the world in all formats, possessing an all-time list of myriads of great athletes, some of whom will be disclosed below.
  • Also known as "The Windies".
  • Have won the ODI world cup twice (1975, 1979).
  • Have won the T20 world cup twice (2012, 2016).
Notable Players / Moments

Sir Vivian Richards

As cricket moved towards a more organized structure and towards a more global reach, the platform was set for the first modern superstar of the game: Vivian Richards. The shining jewel of that great West Indies team which won the first two World Cups, he was unequivocally the best player in the world at the time. Universally admired and with god-given talent, his influence remains strong even today. Widely regarded as one of the three greatest players of all time (along with Sir Don Bradman and Sachin Tendulkar), it isn't too bold to claim that, as the cliché goes: "they don't make them like Sir Viv anymore".

Christopher Henry Gayle

When the first ever cricket World Cup was organized, West Indies were the stars. It felt only right then, that half a century later, when a new format was introduced into the game along with a World Cup of its own in T20s, that West Indies were at the forefront. If Sir Vivian is an embodiment of a cricketing era gone by, Chris Gayle is the embodiment of the modern game. A serial entertainer, he took to the T20 format like a fish to water. He had the build of an athlete and the charisma of a pop-star — in other words, he was the perfect poster boy for this new, exciting form of the game. But above all, Gayle was known for his power-hitting. A bowler's worst nightmare was facing an inform Chris Gayle and when the weather forecast predicted a “Gayle Storm,” even the greatest would run for cover. One of the only men to score a double-century in an ODI and a two times T20 World Cup winner, Chris Gayle's most iconic knock came in the IPL, where some might say he truly garnered his reputation. In what is undoubtedly the greatest Gayle Storm of all time, watch as the boy from Jamaica smashed an unbeaten 175 runs — another record we truly believe will never be broken.

The South African Cricket Team

The South African national cricket team are almost always everybody's second team. Universally loved and extremely entertaining, they have never been able to get their hands on a major ICC honor, but if success were measured in how many hearts a team has won, South Africa would be at the top of the pile.
  • Also known as "The Proteas".
  • No major honors (but did win the Champions Trophy in 1998)
  • Historically known for churning out exceptional fielders
Notable Players / Moments

Dale Steyn

South Africa's all time highest wicket taker, Dale Steyn was simply one of the best to do it. Widely regarded as the greatest test bowler of his generation, he racked up numerous awards including Test Player of the Year in 2008, an award historically difficult for bowlers to win. He also impressively holds the record for the longest stint at the top of the test bowling rankings, a spot he occupied for 263 consecutive weeks (A little over five years). The “Steyn Gun,” as the name suggests, is a tag that highlights his greatest asset: pace. Never before has a player combined pace and precision as well as Steyn did. A batsman's greatest nightmare, he is globally loved, much like our next player…

Abraham Benjamin De Villiers

AB De Villiers, or ABD, is indescribable. No one paragraph can encapsulate the genius of this man, who excelled at every aspect of the game, on and off the field. A favorite of not only Proteas fans, but even neutrals, he is renowned for his ability to play shots all over the field, earning him the tag of “Mr. 360°.” From being a top class finisher to a first rate fielder, he was also no slouch behind the wickets, performing the role of wicket-keeper on multiple occasions for South Africa and in franchise cricket. He holds the record for the fastest ever ODI century, which came in just 31 balls. He famously joined forces with Virat Kohli and Chris Gayle, the two other standout players of the T20 format in his generation, as they teamed up to represent the Royal Challengers Bangalore. Although they never won a major honor as a team, they left the cricketing memories with some very fond memories.

The Pakistani Cricket Team

Pakistan have scary bowlers and decent batsmen. Wasim Akram, Shoaib Akhtar are the goats.
  • Also known as "The Men in Green".
  • Have won the ODI world cup once (1992).
  • Have won the T20 world cup once (2009).
  • Following the 2009 attack on the Sri Lankan national team, no team was allowed to enter Pakistan until 2019.
  • Pakistan Super League - kinda sucks but still exists.
  • Decades-long rivalry with arch-nemesis India.
Notable Players / Moments

Shahid Afridi

Shahid Afridi burst onto the scene like few. In his second ever international game in 1996, he broke the record for the fastest ever ODI century at the time, scoring his ton in 37 balls and establishing his prowess as a lights out power hitter. This is a tag he would never lose until the very end of his playing days to the extent that as long as Afridi was still playing, there was always hope. Following the 1992 World Cup winning team was always going to be an uphill task, and at times Afridi would, with his skillful bowling and expert batting, put the team on his back and more often than not, lead them to victory. One of the most memorable captains of the Pakistan national team, Afridi's impact on the sport in his home country is unlikely to be forgotten.

Wasim Akram

Pakistan have always had a reputation of nurturing deadly fast bowlers, but that reputation didn't come from nowhere. It came from Wasim Akram. Considered one of the greatest fast bowlers of all time, he was the first bowler to reach 500 wickets in ODIs. A World Cup winner and ICC Hall of Famer, he very impressively recorded over four hat-tricks in international cricket, only one of two players to do so. During the aforementioned World Cup in 1992, Akram would take more wickets than any other bowler and guide Pakistan to their only ever ODI World Cup win. He remained at the top of the game for almost his entire career and famously had a great rivalry with Sachin Tendulkar, the standout batsmen from Pakistan's historical rivals: India.
